July 2018

Designed myself a logo to brand my identity, and to practice my design skills.

Just like actors have their stage names, writers have their pen names, as a designer I would like to have my own identity brandings as well, and I think in order to visually attract people, as well as offer myself a design exercise, I decided to design myself a logo.

The logo that’s used on my previous personal website is a light pink logo, which is not very recognizable from a distance, and works only on a dark background.

The logo I used for my old website.

Because I decide to redesign the appearance of my personal website, I need to redo the logo as well.

Procedure

Parallel prototyping is considered to be critical in staying creative and not getting stuck in one single concept.

Since this is a personal website, I started by exploring around my name, specifically my initials. “Huayun Huang” is a Chinese name, so “HYH”, “HHY” and “HH” are all legit initials. Note both H and Y are symmetric letters.

Low-Fi and Mid-Fi logo sketches.

I then selected two favorite designs from the medium fidelity logo, and explore them further with color variations. I think the first one is interesting in that it looks like two people shaking hands with each other; the second one looks better aesthetically, and my initials "HH" are more obvious.

I tried coloring both with my website accent colors, with a little bit of variations for the contrast issue.

I decided that, after all, this is my personal portfolio website, and I want to show my graphic skills. I prefer to depicted as a fresh and energetic designer,instead of some business entity trying to trade with others, so I went with the “H-H” one, which I thought would appeal more to my employer personas.

The HH one on the right wins. Despite of the fact that the left one is more “meaningful” and “friendly”, the one on the right is more visually pleasing and impressive as a PERSONAL website logo. However, if I were designing for a business context, I will pick the left one, for sure.

The logo under different background.

I checked the performance of this logo under several different background (white, #f0f0f0, #333). I think they all looked satisfactory to me.
